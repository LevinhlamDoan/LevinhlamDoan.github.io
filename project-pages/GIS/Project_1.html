Project Details / Background
The project aims to demonstrate the usefulness of a GIS approach to explore the spatial connections between migration flow, house price level, age and distance. Based on the 2011 migration flow data and the 2011 house price data in Greater Manchester in the UK, the paper conducted the Chi-Square tests and produced a series of visual maps to show the spatial relationship between migration flow, distance, age and migration areas segmented by house price levels. The results indicated the strong spatial linkages between migration areas based on the consideration of house price ranges either with age groups, distance ranges or previous locations associated with particular house price levels. Policymakers and planners could use this GIS approach to identify specific migration patterns and areas and help them make informed decisions.
The paper used the boundaries of Output Areas (8,684 areas), Merged Wards (215 areas) from the 2011 UK Census data and the 2011 house price data (with 25,872 postcodes) from the Land Registry data source in Greater Manchester. Furthermore, the paper employed the migration flow data from the 2011 UK Census, and the dataset were downloadable at https://wicid.ukdataservice.ac.uk/. The data included the information about the migration flows from previous areas (one year before) to current destination areas at the levels of output areas and merged wards in Greater Manchester.
